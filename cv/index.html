Publications

  • 2025.05.05
    TrackNetV4: Enhancing Fast Sports Object Tracking with Motion Attention Maps
    To Appear in ICASSP 2025
    This paper introduces TrackNetV4, an enhancement of the TrackNet series for tracking high-speed objects like tennis balls and shuttlecocks. It addresses challenges in partial occlusion and low visibility by combining visual features with learnable motion attention maps through a motion-aware fusion mechanism. This method emphasizes key motion areas, improving tracking accuracy significantly. Experimental results confirm that TrackNetV4 enhances the performance of TrackNetV2 and V3 on relevant datasets.
  • 2024.12.11
    Advancing Video Anomaly Detection: A Concise Review and a New Dataset
    The Thirty-eight Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems Datasets and Benchmarks Track, 2024
    This paper reviews Video Anomaly Detection (VAD), focusing on its applications and the need for concise resources for researchers. It highlights the importance of dataset quality in model performance and introduces the Multi-Scenario Anomaly Detection (MSAD) dataset, featuring 14 diverse scenarios. This dataset aims to improve anomaly detection across varying conditions and supports recent model analyses to address VAD challenges effectively.
  • 2022.01.21
    Comparing Computer Resource Usage Through Interpolating Global Ecosystem Dynamics Investigation Light Detection and Ranging Waveform Data
    International Conference for Advancement in Technology (ICONAT)
    This study compares the computational resources used by cubic and linear splines for modeling waveforms with NASA's GEDI LiDAR data. It evaluates the efficiency and applicability of these interpolation methods for GEDI and similar datasets, aiming to guide researchers in model creation and efficiency for large-scale artificial intelligence applications.
